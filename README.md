## About Me

I am a lecturer and researcher who enjoys building practical software experiments, research pipelines, and reproducible academic workflows. My work sits around computer graphics, real-time simulation, collision detection, crowd behaviour, visibility analysis, and AI-assisted research development.

Beyond code and research, I also enjoy running, gaming, teaching, mentoring students, and turning complex ideas into usable systems.

---

## Current Research Direction

- Shape-aware crowd simulation and ORCA-based movement modelling
- BVH, broad-phase and narrow-phase collision detection
- Visibility, occlusion culling, camera paths, and scene evaluation
- Reproducible experiment design for computer graphics research
- Agentic AI workflows for academic writing, validation, benchmarking, and publication planning
